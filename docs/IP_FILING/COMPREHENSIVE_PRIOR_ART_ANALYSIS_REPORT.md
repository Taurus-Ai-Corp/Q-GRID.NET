## 2. USPTO PATENT FILING REQUIREMENTS (2025)

### Provisional Patent Application Requirements

**Source:** [USPTO Provisional Application Guidance](https://www.uspto.gov/patents/basics/apply/provisional-application)

#### Required Documents

1. **Cover Sheet (Form PTO/SB/16)** - Auto-generated by USPTO portal
2. **Specification** - Written description of invention
3. **Drawings** (if necessary) - Architecture diagrams, flowcharts
4. **Filing Fee** - $70 (micro-entity), $140 (small entity), $280 (large entity)

#### Micro-Entity Status Requirements

**75% fee reduction** if you meet ALL criteria:

- ✅ Qualify as small entity (≤500 employees)
- ✅ Have not been named as inventor on >4 previously filed patent applications
- ✅ Did not have gross income >3× median household income ($234,900 for 2025)
- ✅ Have not assigned rights to entity that doesn't qualify

**TAURUS Eligibility:** Likely qualifies for micro-entity status

### 2025 Electronic Filing Requirements

**DOCX Format Surcharge:** As of January 17, 2024, USPTO charges surcharge for specifications submitted in PDF instead of DOCX format.

**Recommendation:** Convert markdown patent documents to DOCX for filing to avoid surcharge.

**Source:** [USPTO Legal Framework PES 2025](https://www.uspto.gov/sites/default/files/documents/2025LegalFrameworkPES.pdf)

### Blockchain & Fintech Patent Eligibility (2025 Update)

**Major Development:** USPTO Director John Squires (confirmed September 26, 2025) has **lowered § 101 barriers** for AI and software patents.

**Prior Challenge:** Between 2013-2023, 560 blockchain patent applications rejected under Section 101 (abstract idea exclusion).

**Current Landscape:** USPTO making it **easier to get patents** for transformative technology including blockchain and AI.

**Key Requirements for Patentability:**

1. **Demonstrate Technical Improvement:** Show improvement to computer efficiency, not just abstract idea
2. **Solve Real-World Problems:** Patent application must address concrete problems
3. **Specific Implementation:** Focus on specific technical implementations vs abstract concepts

**Examples of Patentable Claims:**

- ✅ Example 47 (USPTO): Artificial neural network for anomaly detection (improvement to computer technology)
- ✅ Quantum-resistant cryptographic integration (technical improvement over classical crypto)
