## BOC'S STATED REASONS FOR SHELVING CBDC

### Reason 1: High Financial Inclusion (99%+ Banked)

**BOC Argument**:
- Canada has among the world's highest financial inclusion rates
- 99%+ of adults have access to banking services
- Payment alternatives already exist (Interac, e-Transfer, credit cards)
- CBDC wouldn't materially improve access

**Reality Check** (BOC Overlooked - Updated Dec 2025):
- **Indigenous financial exclusion**: AFN reports 457 First Nation communities lack adequate internet; only 35% of on-reserve households have 50/10 Mbps (CRTC)
- **363 communities** lack BOTH broadband AND cellular service
- **Infrastructure gap**: Estimated $5.2 billion needed to close (AFN data)
- **Rural payment gaps**: Northern Canada's payment costs 2-3x higher
- **Cross-border pain**: USD/CAD conversion costs 3-5%+ (CBDC could eliminate)

### Reason 2: Existing Payment Methods Sufficient

**BOC Argument**:
- Interac handles 6.5B debit transactions/year
- e-Transfer enables 1.16B digital payments/year
- Card networks provide instant settlement
- No critical gaps requiring CBDC solution

**Reality Check** (Technology Evolution):
- **Interac**: No offline capability, no quantum readiness
- **e-Transfer**: 24-hour delays, $2,000 limit/day, no international
- **Cards**: Internet-dependent, high fraud rates (99.0% vs 99.73% possible)
- **Cross-border**: No real-time USD/CAD settlement
- **Quantum threat**: No roadmap for post-2027 crypto migration

### Reason 3: Regulatory & Policy Complexity

**BOC Argument**:
- CBDC raises privacy concerns
- Provincial jurisdiction conflicts (financial services)
- Complex monetary policy implications
- Risk of cryptocurrency association (BOC doesn't want)

**Reality Check** (Complexity ≠ Impossibility):
- Other G7 countries building CBDCs despite similar concerns
- China's CBDC (e-Yuan) deployed despite authoritarianism concerns
- EU's CBDC (digital EUR) proceeding despite privacy questions
- BOC overstated "complexity" compared to international precedent
