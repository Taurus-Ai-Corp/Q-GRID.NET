# Community Advisory Board Recruitment Package

## Purpose

Establish an Indigenous-majority Community Advisory Board to govern Q-GRID's deployment in First Nations communities. This board ensures technology decisions reflect Indigenous priorities, cultural protocols, and data sovereignty requirements.

---

## Board Overview

### Mission Statement

*"To ensure Q-GRID's quantum-safe payment infrastructure serves Indigenous communities according to Indigenous values, with Indigenous leadership, and under Indigenous governance."*

## Roles & Responsibilities

### All Advisory Board Members

**Time Commitment**: 4-6 hours/month
- Monthly meetings (2 hours, virtual/hybrid)
- Document review between meetings (1-2 hours)
- Occasional community consultation participation

**Responsibilities**:
1. Attend monthly Advisory Board meetings
2. Review and approve major product decisions
3. Ensure cultural appropriateness of Q-GRID deployment
4. Represent community perspectives honestly
5. Escalate concerns from communities
6. Guide data sovereignty implementation
7. Advise on expansion to new communities

### Chair (Indigenous Leader)

**Additional Responsibilities**:
- Set meeting agendas with Q-GRID support
- Facilitate consensus-based decision-making
- Serve as primary spokesperson (if desired)
- Mediate any disputes between stakeholders
- Champion Indigenous priorities in all decisions

**Ideal Profile**:
- Recognized Indigenous leader (chief, councillor, or equivalent experience)
- Understanding of financial services and technology
- Commitment to Indigenous data sovereignty
- Ability to bridge Indigenous and corporate perspectives
- Strong facilitation and consensus-building skills

### Community Representatives (3 positions)

**Responsibilities**:
- Represent their specific community's interests
- Communicate board decisions to community leadership
- Gather community feedback on Q-GRID service
- Identify cultural protocols for their community
- Participate in community co-design sessions

**Ideal Profile**:
- Chief, council member, or chief-designated representative
- Trusted voice within their community
- Interest in financial technology and innovation
- Ability to translate technical concepts for community members

### FNBC Representative

**Responsibilities**:
- Represent FNBC partnership perspective
- Advise on banking regulatory requirements
- Facilitate community connections through FNBC network
- Ensure alignment with FNBC values and policies

**Ideal Profile**:
- Senior FNBC staff member (Director level or above)
- Indigenous (preferred) or demonstrated Indigenous allyship
- Knowledge of Indigenous financial services landscape
- Authority to represent FNBC position

### Indigenous Fintech Expert

**Responsibilities**:
- Provide technical expertise on fintech and payments
- Advise on Indigenous technology sovereignty models
- Connect board to broader Indigenous tech community
- Challenge assumptions and ensure innovation

**Ideal Profile**:
- Experience in fintech, payments, or financial services
- Indigenous identity or deep Indigenous community ties
- Understanding of blockchain, quantum computing, or related tech
- Track record of Indigenous advocacy

### Elder/Cultural Advisor

**Responsibilities**:
- Ensure cultural protocols are respected
- Advise on traditional governance alignment
- Provide wisdom and long-term perspective
- Guide appropriate community engagement approaches

**Ideal Profile**:
- Recognized Elder or cultural knowledge keeper
- Respected voice in Indigenous communities
- Understanding of traditional decision-making processes
- Patience for technology learning curve

## Governance Framework

### Decision-Making Process

**Consensus-Based Approach:**
1. Q-GRID presents proposal or issue
2. Open discussion among all members
3. Chair seeks consensus
4. If consensus not reached, concerns documented
5. Modified proposal presented
6. Repeat until consensus or escalate to FNBC steering committee

**Voting (if consensus fails):**
- Simple majority of voting members
- Chair breaks ties
- Dissenting views recorded in minutes

### Meeting Structure

**Frequency**: Monthly (12 meetings/year)
**Duration**: 2 hours
**Format**: Hybrid (virtual + in-person rotation)

**Standard Agenda**:
1. Opening/Acknowledgment (5 min)
2. Previous minutes approval (5 min)
3. Q-GRID operational update (15 min)
4. Community feedback review (20 min)
5. Decision items (45 min)
6. Discussion items (20 min)
7. Next steps and action items (5 min)
8. Closing (5 min)

### Quorum Requirements

- Minimum 5 voting members present
- Must include Chair (or designated alternate)
- Must include at least 2 community representatives

### Conflict of Interest

- Members must declare any conflicts
- Conflicted members recuse from relevant decisions
- All declarations recorded in minutes

## Recruitment Process

### Timeline

| Phase | Timeline | Activities |
|-------|----------|------------|
| Outreach | Dec 2025 - Jan 2026 | Identify candidates, initial conversations |
| Recruitment | Jan 2026 | Formal invitations, confirmations |
| Onboarding | Feb 2026 | Orientation, first meeting |
| Operational | Feb 2026+ | Regular monthly meetings |

### Candidate Identification

**Sources**:
- FNBC leadership recommendations
- Pilot community chief nominations
- NACCA network referrals
- Indigenous innovation networks (ICTC, Digital Nations)
- Assembly of First Nations connections

### Outreach Approach

**Principle**: Relationship before transaction

1. **Initial Introduction**: Warm introduction via trusted contact
2. **Informal Conversation**: No commitment discussion about opportunity
3. **Information Sharing**: Provide this recruitment package
4. **Questions & Concerns**: Address any hesitations
5. **Formal Invitation**: Written invitation with terms
6. **Confirmation**: Written acceptance

### Onboarding Process

**Pre-First Meeting**:
- Welcome package with all Q-GRID documentation
- One-on-one orientation call with Q-GRID team
- Technology setup assistance (if needed)
- Introduction to other board members

**First Meeting**:
- Extended time for introductions
- Q-GRID technology demonstration
- Review of governance framework
- Discussion of priorities and concerns
- Set first-year objectives
